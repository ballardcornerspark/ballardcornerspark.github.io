Fundraising in 2007

2006 has been a big year for the park with the initial plans and grants starting the process for real.

2007, however, is the year where we need to raise money to make the park happen.

With that in mind we have now added the Fundraising page to the website, which we plan to update at least quarterly with our “official” figures – the December 2006 figures are included just now. More regular posts will report progress as it happens. The main page also sports a fundraising strip – $171,273 down, $269,727 to go.

There is also a Timeline page, detailing our plans for the next year, and which will have our fundraising goals highlighted.

The Paypal-based “Ballard Corners Park” merchandise shop is in construction, which will allow t-shirts and buttons to be purchased online – but in the meantime all members of the committee should feel free to sell or distribute them as appropriate ($10/shirt, $1/button) and contact David or Gordon for the goods.

With that in mind, it will always help to drive friends, family and interested people to the site. The best links to use are:

  • http://ballardcornerspark.org – the main site link
  • http://ballardcornerspark.org/shop – the main shopping link where we receive a percentage of the revenue
  • http://ballardcornerspark.org/donate – a simple Paypal donation page, with instructions on how to send checks

Feel free to circulate these links, add them to your own website and blogs, or use them as signatures on your email with catchy slogans such as these:

---
Help build a park: shop Amazon at http://BallardCornersPark.org/Shop
(a percentage of proceeds will contribute to the park)

Disappointment and Hope

There was disappointment yesterday as Governor Gregoire did not include funding for Ballard Corners Park (BCP) in her proposed budget.

Gabriella Moller, Susan O’Neill, and I met with Senators Jeanne Kohl-Welles and Karen Fraser. Senator Fraser writes the capital budget in the Senate. She listened intently to our presentation and encouraged us to apply for competitive grants, which we will. She described an uphill battle to get funding for BCP included in her capital budget.

Later, the two senators drove by the site on the way back from another meeting.
Sen. Kohl-Welles continues to be a wonderful advocate for BCP. She will continue to work with Sen. Fraser on our behalf. I may meet with Sen. Fraser again and testify in front of the Ways and Means Committee with the session starts in January.

One door closes, another opens

Recently, I wrote that I was going to meet with Hans Dunshee, who was, at the time, the Chair of the Washington State House Capital Projects Committee. However, since the recent election swept more Democrats into the state legislature, some changes have occurred.
For reasons beyond me, Dunshee slipped over to the Appropriations Committee to be number two behind the venerable local representative Helen Sommers. I suspect Dunshee saw it as a promotion, but I don’t know. It does put him right behind Sommers in seniority and should she decide to retire, he’d be in position to control some nice purse strings.

Now Rep. Bill Fromhold (from Vancouver, Washington) is the new chair of the Capital Budgets Committee.
I canceled my meeting with Dunshee and will meet with Fromhold on 4 Jan in Olympia.

Ballard District Council Holiday Party

I attended the Ballard District Council Holiday Party last night.  They gave out a bunch of awards and as I was standing there, a woman I’d never met before said to me, “Are you accepting contributions tonight?”  I, trying to act non-plussed, said, “Yes!”  When I looked at the name on the $100 check, she turned out to be Diana Aakervik, the wife of Warren Aakervik, owner of Ballard Oil.  I thanked her and retrieved some buttons for the both of them.
